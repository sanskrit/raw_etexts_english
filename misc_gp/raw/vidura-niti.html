“A man on a destructive path exhibits the following eight tendencies: He is jealous of the Brahmins; he courts their enmity; he usurps their wealth and desires to kill them. (श्लोक-101-103)

“O BhŒrata! Look for these eight happenings which indicate that people are happy. The same are in themselves a source of joy in this world—1. Social gatherings and interaction of friends, 2. (श्लोक-104)

अष्टौ गुणा: पुरुषं दीपयन्ति

(श्लोक-108)

“A king who eschews lust, gives up anger, distributes wealth to the deserving, who is learned in scriptures, is knowledgeable, and performs his duty expeditiously, sets an example for others to emulate. (109)

जानाति विश्वासयितुं मनुष्यान्

“Riches flow into the coffers of a king who instils faith in his subjects, the one who awards punishment only to those whose guilt has been established, who is aware of the limits of the maximum and the minimum sentence, and the one who understands when to pardon and when not. (110)

सुदुर्बलं नावजानाति कञ्चिद्

“It is a courageous and resolute ruler who does not insult the weak, who treats even an enemy carefully but intelligently, who avoids a conflict with the powerful, and who fights chivalrously when the occasion so arises. (111)

(श्लोक-112)

“A person is always happy if he avoids unnecessary travel, companionship of men of easy virtue, if he does not covet another man’s wife, shuns the evils of falsehood, arrogance, and hypocrisy, does not steal, back-bite or consume liquor. (113)

“A person possessed of the following attributes earns approbation amongst all—1. He does not initiate an effort in Dharma, Artha or KŒma in anger or in haste, 2. न पौरुषेणापि विकत्थतेऽन्यान्।

“A person who does not try to look formidable all the time, who does not all along brag to others about his chivalry and mite, one who even though highly upset and angry, does not utter unpleasant words, endears himself to everyone. (116)

(श्लोक-117)

न वैरमुद्दीपयति प्रशान्तं

न दर्पमारोहति नास्तमेति।

न दुर्गतोऽस्मीति करोत्यकार्यं

“The noble [the rya] consider a person of good conduct to be superior if he does not fan the extinguished embers of enmity, does neither act arrogant nor mean, and does not act foul and behave undesirably under the pretext that he is in grave danger. (117)

(श्लोक-118)

“Amongst persons of noble conduct he is considered superior to others who is not all the time celebrating his good fortune, who does not rejoice over the others’ misfortune, and who never feels sorry over what he has given away in charity. (118)

(श्लोक-119)

देशाचारान् समयाञ्जातिधर्मान्

बुभूषते य: स परावरज्ञ:।

स यत्र तत्राभिगत: सदैव

“A person who makes an effort to learn about the habits, social conduct and Dharma of his countrymen, soon learns to distinguish between the desirable and the undesirable. Such a person, wherever he goes, is able to establish his hold over the masses. (119)

(श्लोक-120)

दम्भं मोहं मत्सरं पापकृत्यं

राजद्विष्टं पैशुनं पूगवैरम्।

“That wise man alone is superior to others who gives up arrogance, attachment, evil deeds, envy, treachery, back-biting, enmity with the other social groups and contentious arguments with the persons who have lost their mind or with persons of evil intent. (120)

(श्लोक-121)

दानं होमं दैवतं मङ्गलानि

“The DevatŒs pray for the prosperity of the person who gives charity, performs havana, worships gods, performs auspicious deeds, repents for his mistakes or omissions, and daily attends to the most desirable activities. (121)

“A learned man’s conduct is praiseworthy if he marries, makes friends, interacts socially and engages in conversation among equals. He shuns those who are not worthy of his association. मितं भुङ्‍क्ते संविभज्याश्रितेभ्यो

“A thinking person automatically gets rid of miseries who partakes of whatever is left after distributing the food among his dependents, who works more and sleeps less, and who gives away a part of his wealth to the needy who is not even his friend. (123)

“One ought to be ever ready to be at peace and conciliatory with others. One should be truthful, gentle and respectful towards others. Let one’s thoughts be pure. A person with these attributes shines like a glistening, highly valuable gem from a reputed mine among his class. (125)

(श्लोक-17)

यथा मधु समादत्ते रक्षन् पुष्पाणि षट्पद:।

तद्वदर्थान्मनुष्येभ्य आदद्यादविहिंसया॥

“Like the black bee which sucks the sap of a flower while protecting it, a king gathers taxes painlessly from his subjects. (17)

(श्लोक-18)

पुष्पं पुष्पं विचिन्वीत मूलच्छेदं न कारयेत्।

मालाकार इवारामे न यथाङ्गारकारक:॥

“A gardener plucks flowers but does not pull out the roots of the plant. Similarly, a king gathers taxes in order to provide protection to the public. He does not uproot them like the person making charcoal out of it. (18)

(श्लोक-19)

किन्नु मे स्यादिदं कृत्वा किन्नु मे स्यादकुर्वत:।

इति कर्माणि संचिन्त्य कुर्याद् वा पुरुषो न वा॥

“Carefully consider an action to its end. Will it be beneficial or harmful? A decision to perform a task should be based on such considerations. (19)

(श्लोक-20)

अनारभ्या भवन्त्यर्था: केचिन्नित्यं तथागता:।

कृत: पुरुषकारो हि भवेद् येषु निरर्थक:॥

“Certain unnecessary and uncommon tasks are never accomplished. These are not worth even a try. Human effort is wasted in trying to perform such tasks. (20)

(श्लोक-21)

प्रसादो निष्फलो यस्य क्रोधश्चापि निरर्थक:।

न तं भर्तारमिच्छन्ति षण्ढं पतिमिव स्त्रिय:॥

“A king who cannot be generous when pleased and who cannot be tough even when angry, is as much unwanted by the public as an impotent man by a woman. (21)

(श्लोक-22)

कांश्चिदर्थान्नर: प्राज्ञो लघुमूलान्महाफलान्।

क्षिप्रमारभते कर्तुं न विघ्नयति तादृशान्॥

“A wise man does not delay performing a task where with little effort a significant result can be achieved. He does not brook any hurdle in such activity. (22)

(श्लोक-23)

ऋजु पश्यति य: सर्वं चक्षुषानुपिबन्निव।

आसीनमपि तूष्णीकमनुरज्यन्ति तं प्रजा:॥

“A king who looks kindly at his subjects, looks at them with such concentration as if he would drink them through his eyes into his heart, is loved by his PrajŒ even if he is not very voluble. (23)

(श्लोक-24)

सुपुष्पित: स्यादफल: फलित: स्याद् दुरारुह:।

अपक्व: पक्वसंकाशो न तु शीर्येत कर्हिचित्॥

“A king should not be easily accessible. He ought to be like a flower-laden tree which does not bear fruits. He should be like a fruit-laden tree which is not easy to climb. Even though weak, he must exude confidence and strength. Such a king cannot be uprooted. (24)

(श्लोक-25)

चक्षुषा मनसा वाचा कर्मणा च चतुर्विधम्।

प्रसादयति यो लोकं तं लोकोऽनुप्रसीदति॥

“A king makes his subjects happy if he serves them attentively, wholeheartedly, addresses them courteously and works for their welfare. [Literally, ‘serves his subjects with his eyes, mind, words and deeds.’] (25)

(श्लोक-26)

यस्मात् त्रस्यन्ति भूतानि मृगव्याधान्मृगा इव।

सागरान्तामपि महीं लब्ध्वा स परिहीयते॥

“A king must not strike terror in the heart of his subjects just as a hunter frightens a deer. Such a ruler even though he has conquered the world, is soon deserted by his public. (26)

(श्लोक-27)

पितृपैतामहं राज्यं प्राप्तवान् स्वेन कर्मणा।

वायुरभ्रमिवासाद्य भ्रंशयत्यनये स्थित:॥

“Like the winds scattering the clouds hither and thither, an unjust king destroys with his misdeeds even an inherited kingdom. (27)

(श्लोक-28)

धर्ममाचरतो राज्ञ: सद्भिश्चरितमादित:।

वसुधा वसुसम्पूर्णा वर्धते भूतिवर्धिनी॥

“If a king follows the righteous norms traditionally well-established by the wise, his empire prospers, the earth under his control produces more, and his wealth multiplies. With this happening, his glory and prestige scale new heights. (28)

(श्लोक-29)

अथ संत्यजतो धर्ममधर्मं चानुतिष्ठत:।

प्रतिसंवेष्टते भूमिरग्नौ चर्माहितं यथा॥

“When a king deserting the path of Dharma, takes to the path of Adharma, his empire shrinks like a sheet of leather placed on fire. (29)

(श्लोक-30)

य एव यत्न: क्रियते परराष्ट्रविमर्दने।

स एव यत्न: कर्तव्य: स्वराष्ट्रपरिपालने॥

“Save the effort that is needed to destroy another kingdom and utilize that energy for the uplift of your kingdom. (30)

(श्लोक-31)

धर्मेण राज्यं विन्देत धर्मेण परिपालयेत्।

धर्ममूलां श्रियं प्राप्य न जहाति न हीयते॥

“Establish a kingdom in Dharma and protect it accordingly. A kingdom righteously attained does not slip out of a king’s hands nor does a king let it go. (31)

(श्लोक-32)

अप्युन्मत्तात् प्रलपतो बालाच्च परिजल्पत:।

सर्वत: सारमादद्यादश्मभ्य इव काञ्चनम्॥

“Like extracting gold out of rocks, one must learn something of substance even from a child who babbles, and an insane who talks aimlessly. (32)

(श्लोक-33)

सुव्याहृतानि सूक्तानि सुकृतानि ततस्तत:।

संचिन्वन् धीर आसीत शिलाहारी शिलं यथा॥३३॥

“Like a ascetic who depends on small gleanings of grain even from a rock, a man of substance gathers knowledge from all sources such as thoughtful sayings, intellectual writings as well as from watching and emulating the good deeds of others. (33)

(श्लोक-34)

गन्धेन गाव: पश्यन्ति वेदै: पश्यन्ति ब्राह्मणा:।

चारै: पश्यन्ति राजानश्चक्षुर्भ्यामितरे जना:॥

“Its sense of smell leads a cow. A Brahmin follows the Vedic percepts. A king keeps an eye on his kingdom through his secret agents. The ordinary folk use their eyes to perceive. (34)

(श्लोक-35)

भूयांसं लभते क्लेशं या गौर्भवति दुर्दुहा।

अथ या सुदुहा राजन् नैव तां वितुदन्त्यपि॥

“RŒjan! A cow which does not easily yield her milk, suffers discomfort. The one that allows itself to be milked easily is not maltreated. (35)

(श्लोक-36)

यदतप्तं प्रणमति न तत् संतापयन्त्यपि।

यच्च स्वयं नतं दारु न तत् संनमयन्त्यपि॥

“A malleable piece of metal does not require a furnace. A bent wooden stick needs no further effort to bend it. (36)

(श्लोक-37)

एतयोपमया धीर: संनमेत बलीयसे।

इन्द्राय स प्रणमते नमते यो बलीयसे॥

“All these illustrations indicate that the wise bow in the presence of the mighty. Giving in to a stronger person is like bowing before Indra. (37)

(श्लोक-38)

पर्जन्यनाथा: पशवो राजानो मन्त्रिबान्धवा:।

पतयो बान्धवा: स्त्रीणां ब्राह्मणा वेदबान्धवा:॥

“The clouds protect the animals. The ministers assist a king. The husbands look after their wives. The Brahmins derive support from the Vedas. (38)

(श्लोक-39)

सत्येन रक्ष्यते धर्मो विद्या योगेन रक्ष्यते।

मृजया रक्ष्यते रूपं कुलं वृत्तेन रक्ष्यते॥

“The truth protects Dharma while concentration and application preserves learning. Constant care protects a person’s charm and noble conduct protects a family. (39)

(श्लोक-40)

मानेन रक्ष्यते धान्यमश्वान् रक्षत्यनुक्रम:।

अभीक्ष्णदर्शनं गाश्च स्त्रियो रक्ष्या: कुचैलत:॥

“Correct weight and measurement and care protect the grain. The horses need to exercise all the time. The cows need constant care. Dirty clothes offer protection to women by hiding their charms. (40)

(श्लोक-41)

न कुलं वृत्तहीनस्य प्रमाणमिति मे मति:।

अन्तेष्वपि हि जातानां वृत्तमेव विशिष्यते॥

“In my view, birth in a noble family is no armour for a characterless man. A man though born low is superior if he is of a sound moral character. (41)

(श्लोक-42)

य ईर्षु: परवित्तेषु रूपे वीर्ये कुलान्वये।

सुखसौभाग्यसत्कारे तस्य व्याधिरनन्तक:॥

“A man suffers from an incurable malady if he is envious of others’ wealth, beauty, valour, social status, happiness, good fortune, and prestige. (42)

(श्लोक-43)

अकार्यकरणाद् भीत: कार्याणां च विवर्जनात्।

अकाले मन्त्रभेदाच्च येन माद्येन्न तत् पिबेत्॥

“One should be afraid of undertaking an undesirable task, one should be afraid of delay in what is to be accomplished and leakage of secrets yet to be achieved. One should not imbibe intoxicating liquor. (43)

(श्लोक-44)

विद्यामदो धनमदस्तृतीयोऽभिजनो मद:।

मदा एतेऽवलिप्तानामेत एव सतां दमा:॥

“For an arrogant person boasting of his education, wealth and his family standing, can be poisonous. [It can result in an unseemly behaviour and undesirable consequences.] However, for the noble, these attributes are a source of strength [to do good.] (44)

(श्लोक-45)

असन्तोऽभ्यर्थिता: सद्भि: क्वचित्कार्ये कदाचन।

मन्यन्ते सन्तमात्मानमसन्तमपि विश्रुतम्॥

“Even a villain takes airs and considers himself a virtuous person in case he is approached by a noble person with a request. (45)

(श्लोक-46)

गतिरात्मवतां सन्त: सन्त एव सतां गति:।

असतां च गति: सन्तो न त्वसन्त: सतां गति:॥

“The saintly persons provide moral support to the intellectuals as well as to other persons of a saintly disposition. Even the wicked are helped by the saints. However, the reverse never happens. (46)

(श्लोक-47)

जिता सभा वस्त्रवता मिष्टाशा गोमता जिता।

अध्वा जितो यानवता सर्वं शीलवता जितम्॥

“A well dressed person wins over others in an assembly. Anyone possessing a cow can successfully cater to sweet buds. If you possess a carriage, you can comfortably travel long distances. Likewise, a man of noble character easily wins over others. (47)

(श्लोक-48)

शीलं प्रधानं पुरुषे तद् यस्येह प्रणश्यति।

न तस्य जीवितेनार्थो न धनेन न बन्धुभि:॥

“Character is the bed-rock of a human being. If it is destroyed, his life, wealth and his relatives are of no advantage to him. (48)

(श्लोक-49)

आढ्यानां मांसपरमं मध्यानां गोरसोत्तरम्।

तैलोत्तरं दरिद्राणां भोजनं भरतर्षभ॥

“O superior among the Bharatas! The arrogant wealthy take more of meat in their meals while the middle class is happy with milk and milk products. The poor, however, use more of oil than other ingredients while cooking food. (49)

(श्लोक-50)

सम्पन्नतरमेवान्नं दरिद्रा भुञ्जते सदा।

क्षुत् स्वादुतां जनयति सा चाढ्येषु सुदुर्लभा॥

“The paupers always relish their meal because being hungry they enjoy whatever is available. This sort of pleasure is not available to the rich. (50)

(श्लोक-51)

प्रायेण श्रीमतां लोके भोक्तुं शक्तिर्न विद्यते।

जीर्यन्त्यपि हि काष्ठानि दरिद्राणां महीपते॥

“RŒjan! The rich in this world cannot even digest a proper meal [because the tension in their life upsets their digestion.] A poor man can digest even wood pieces. (51)

(श्लोक-52)

अवृत्तिर्भयमन्त्यानां मध्यानां मरणाद् भयम्।

उत्तमानां तु मर्त्यानामवमानात् परं भयम्॥

“A lowly person is worried about unemployment. A middle-class man fears death. The highest fear dishonour more than any other thing. (52)

(श्लोक-53)

ऐश्वर्यमदपापिष्ठा मदा: पानमदादय:।

ऐश्वर्यमदमत्तो हि नापतित्वा विबुध्यते॥

“While liquor certainly intoxicates and is therefore undesirable, the desire to live a luxurious life intoxicates all the more. It is so frightening because a person enamoured of an indulgent life does not return to his senses before his downfall. (53)

(श्लोक-54)

इन्द्रियैरिन्द्रियार्थेषु वर्तमानैरनिग्रहै:।

तैरयं ताप्यते लोको नक्षत्राणि ग्रहैरिव॥

“The world suffers from uncontrolled senses and resultant desires. This results in unbridled indulgence. Everyone suffers thus like the stars diminished in the presence of the Sun. (54)

(श्लोक-55)

यो जित: पञ्चवर्गेण सहजेनात्मकर्षिणा।

आपदस्तस्य वर्धन्ते शुक्लपक्ष इवोडुराट्॥

“The sufferings of a person who is overwhelmed by the five senses of touch, smell, sight, taste and sound, senses which lead the human beings astray, multiply like the waxing moon. (55)

(श्लोक-56)

अविजित्य य आत्मानममात्यान् विजिगीषते।

अमित्रान् वाजितामात्य: सोऽवश: परिहीयते॥

“A king who, without first conquering his five senses and the mind, asks his ministers to comply with his orders, or who, without subordinating his advisers, wants to conquer his enemies, is soon deserted by his public. (56)

(श्लोक-57)

आत्मानमेव प्रथमं द्वेष्यरूपेण यो जयेत्।

ततोऽमात्यानमित्रांश्च न मोघं विजिगीषते॥

“A king who takes them on as his enemies and conquers his five senses and the wandering mind, surely succeeds in being victorious over his ministers as well as his enemies. (57)

(श्लोक-58)

वश्येन्द्रियं जितात्मानं धृतदण्डं विकारिषु।

परीक्ष्य कारिणं धीरमत्यन्तं श्रीर्निषेवते॥

“A sober king who has conquered his senses and his wayward mind, who awards punishment to the guilty and undertakes a task only after weighing the pros and cons, has Lak¦m¶ always at his beck and call. (58)

(श्लोक-59)

रथ: शरीरं पुरुषस्य राजन्

नात्मा नियतेन्द्रियाण्यस्य चाश्वा:।

तैरप्रमत्त: कुशली सदश्वै-

र्दान्तै: सुखं याति रथीव धीर:॥

“RŒjan! The human body is like a chariot of which the soul is the charioteer. The sensory perceptions are the horses geared to pull this chariot. An ever-alert man who has reigned them, who is dexterous, clever and in control of himself, travels on this chariot of life in joy and peace. (59)

(श्लोक-60)

एतान्यनिगृहीतानि व्यापादयितुमप्यलम्।

अविधेया इवादान्ता हया: पथि कुसारथिम्॥

“The senses when not reined are powerful enough to destroy a man just as untamed and uncontrollable horses throw off the foolish charioteer. (60)

(श्लोक-61)

अनर्थमर्थत: पश्यन्नर्थं चैवाप्यनर्थत:।

इन्द्रियैरजितैर्बाल: सुदु:खं मन्यते सुखम्॥

“An ignorant man, unable to control his senses, takes the real for the unreal and accepts the apparent for what might be worthless. Such a man [having lost his sense of discrimination] rejoices even when he is in fact deep in sorrow. (61)

(श्लोक-62)

धर्मार्थौ य: परित्यज्य स्यादिन्द्रियवशानुग:।

श्रीप्राणधनदारेभ्य: क्षिप्रं स परिहीयते॥

“Ignoring Dharma and Artha, such a person becomes a slave to his senses. Soon he gets deprived of his prosperity, his woman, his wealth, and even his life. (62)

(श्लोक-63)

अर्थानामीश्वरो य: स्यादिन्द्रियाणामनीश्वर:।

इन्द्रियाणामनैश्वर्यादैश्वर्याद् भ्रश्यते हि स:॥

“He may be wealthy but if he has not overpowered his senses, his wealth gets destroyed because of his lack of control over senses. (63)

(श्लोक-64)

आत्मनाऽऽत्मानमन्विच्छेन्मनोबुद्धीन्द्रियैर्यतै:।

आत्मा ह्येवात्मनो बन्धुरात्मैव रिपुरात्मन:॥

“Controlling your mind, the five senses as well as your sense of discrimination, try to look within yourself in order to realize your soul. Your soul is your friend as well as your enemy. (64)

(श्लोक-65)

बन्धुरात्माऽऽत्मनस्तस्य येनैवात्माऽऽत्मना जित:।

स एव नियतो बन्धु: स एव नियतो रिपु:॥

“Realization of your soul [of yourself] makes the soul your true friend. It is your well-wisher as well as your enemy. (65)

(श्लोक-66)

क्षुद्राक्षेणेव जालेन झषावपिहितावुरू।

कामश्च राजन् क्रोधश्च तौ प्रज्ञानं विलुम्पत:॥

“RŒjan! As two large fish trapped in a net with tiny holes, working together tear the net apart, lust and anger do away with the sense of discrimination. (66)

(श्लोक-67)

समवेक्ष्येह धर्मार्थौ सम्भारान् योऽधिगच्छति।

स वै सम्भृतसम्भार: सततं सुखमेधते॥

“He who keeping in mind the dictates of Dharma and Artha in this world, organizes the means of achieving his ends, forever stays happy and prosperous with their help. (67)

(श्लोक-68)

य: पंचाभ्यन्तरान् शत्रूनविजित्य मनोमयान्।

जिगीषति रिपूनन्यान् रिपवोऽभिभवन्ति तम्॥

“Anyone who without having conquered his five senses which are basically his weaknesses and therefore inimical to his well-being, tries to conquer his enemies, is defeated. [The unvanquished five senses are enemies within, hidden and dangerous.] (68)

(श्लोक-69)

दृश्यन्ते हि महात्मानो बध्यमाना: स्वकर्मभि:।

इन्द्रियाणामनीशत्वाद् राजानो राज्यविभ्रमै:॥

“In case the five senses are not subjugated, even the great saints become subject to the consequences of their deeds. In a similar situation, the kings stay mired in meaningless lives of luxury to the detriment of their kingdom. (69)

(श्लोक-70)

असंत्यागात् पापकृतामपापां-

स्तुल्यो दण्ड: स्पृशते मिश्रभावात्।

शुष्केणार्द्रं दह्यते मिश्रभावात्

तस्मात् पापै: सह सन्धिं न कुर्यात्॥

“Even an innocent man suffers the consequences of his association with the guilty. It is like water-soaked sticks catching fire easily when tied with a dry pack. Hence, avoid an evil person under all circumstances. (70)

(श्लोक-71)

The HaÅsa replied: “DevatŒs! I have learnt that a person’s duty lies in being composed, in control of the senses and the mind and in following the Truth and the Dharma. Shedding all prejudices, he should treat everyone equally whether he likes him or not. (4)

(श्लोक-5)

आक्रुश्यमानो नाक्रोशेन्मन्युरेव तितिक्षत:।

आक्रोष्टारं निर्दहति सुकृतं चास्य विन्दति॥

“He should not retort to foul words with expletives. Controlling his anger, the person who forgives the guilty, reduces him to nothingness. To him is transferred any merit that might be due to the guilty. (5)

(श्लोक-6)

नाक्रोशी स्यान्नावमानी परस्य

मित्रद्रोही नोत नीचोपसेवी।

न चाभिमानी न च हीनवृत्तो

रूक्षां वाचं रुषतीं वर्जयीत॥

“He must not insult another nor abuse him. He must not betray a friend nor serve another who is mean and vicious. He should not be arrogant nor of a loose moral character. He must avoid use of angry, harsh and unkind words in addressing another. (6)

(श्लोक-7)

मर्माण्यस्थीनि हृदयं तथासून्

रूक्षा वाचो निर्दहन्तीह पुंसाम्।

तस्माद् वाचमुषतीं रूक्षरूपां

धर्मारामो नित्यशो वर्जयीत॥

“Harsh and unkind words hurt the listener in the core of his heart, mind and even bones. Such words make the life of the addressee miserable as if sinned. Therefore, a righteous person should give up the use of harsh words forever. (7)

(श्लोक-8)

अरुन्तुदं परुषं रूक्षवाचं

वाक्कण्टकैर्वितुदन्तं मनुष्यान्।

विद्यादलक्ष्मीकतमं जनानां

मुखे निबद्धां निर्ऋतिं वै वहन्तम्॥

“A person who uses harsh and unbecoming words, who is hot tempered, who hurts others in their weak moments, and who pierces others with poisonous barbs, is like a pauper, or like the dregs in a rubbish heap. [In the form of foul and harsh language] he carries penury and death in his mouth. (8)

(श्लोक-9)

परश्चेदेनमभिविध्येत बाणै-

र्भृशं सुतीक्ष्णैरनलार्कदीप्तै:।

स विध्यमानोऽप्यतिदह्यमानो

विद्यात् कवि: सुकृतं मे दधाति॥

“If a righteous person is injured by another with penetrating arrows of taunts that burn like fire or the rays of the Sun, the learned should take it as if his suffering is adding to his stock of PuÄya [merit.] (9)

(श्लोक-10)

यदि सन्तं सेवति यद्यसन्तं

तपस्विनं यदि वा स्तेनमेव।

वासो यथा रंगवशं प्रयाति

तथा स तेषां वशमभ्युपैति॥

“Like cloth taking on the colour of a dye in which it is soaked, a noble person is adversely affected if he renders service to or keeps the company of an evil person, a fraudulent Tapasv¶ or a thief. His coming under their sway affects his character (10)

(श्लोक-11)

अतिवादं न प्रवदेन्न वादयेद्

योऽनाहत: प्रतिहन्यान्न घातयेत्।

हन्तुं च यो नेच्छति पापकं वै

तस्मै देवा: स्पृहयन्त्यागताय॥

“Even the gods await the arrival of a person who does not speak ill of others or who does not compel others to back-bite. Such a person unless provoked, does not attack others nor does he provoke others to hurt anyone. He is so noble that even if hurt by someone, he forgives the guilty. (11)

(श्लोक-12)

अव्याहृतं व्याहृताच्छ्रेय आहु:

सत्यं वदेद् व्याहृतं तद् द्वितीयम्।

प्रियं वदेद् व्याहृतं तत् तृतीयं

धर्मं वदेद् व्याहृतं तच्चतुर्थम्॥

“It is said that silence is better than speech. However, the second virtue of speech is telling the truth, the first being silence. Use of pleasant words is the third desirable attribute of speech. Fourthly, speaking according to Dharma makes even pleasant words sound better. (12)

(श्लोक-13)

यादृशै: सन्निविशते यादृशांश्चोपसेवते।

यादृगिच्छेच्च भवितुं तादृग् भवति पूरुष:॥

“A human being takes on the colour of the company he keeps or of those he serves. He can mould himself according to his will as well. (13)

(श्लोक-14)

यतो यतो निवर्तते ततस्ततो विमुच्यते।

निवर्तनाद्धि सर्वतो न वेत्ति दु:खमण्वपि॥

“A man is freed from the desires he wants to eschew or take his mind off from. Once completely detached, he is unaffected by any sorrow. (14)

(श्लोक-15)

न जीयते चानुजिगीषतेऽन्या-

न्न वैरकृच्चाप्रतिघातकश्च।

निन्दाप्रशंसासु समस्वभावो

न शोचते हृष्यति नैव चायम्॥

“He moves beyond happiness and sorrow who is neither vanquished nor has a desire to overcome another, who is not inimical to anyone, who does not like to hurt the others, and who looks at infamy and aplomb with equanimity. (15)

(श्लोक-16)

भावमिच्छति सर्वस्य नाभावे कुरुते मन:।

सत्यवादी मृदुर्दान्तो य: स उत्तमपूरुष:॥

“He is a superior being who wishes everyone well, who never nurses an ill-will for anyone else, who is truthful and soft-spoken and who has conquered his senses. (16)

(श्लोक-17)

नानर्थकं सान्त्वयति प्रतिज्ञाय ददाति च।

रन्ध्रं परस्य जानाति य: स मध्यमपूरुष:॥

“That person is slightly lower who does not make a false promise, who delivers what he has promised, and who is aware of others’ shortcomings. (17)

(श्लोक-18)

दु:शासनस्तूपहतोऽभिशस्तो

नावर्तते मन्युवशात् कृतघ्न:।

न कस्यचिन्मित्रमथो दुरात्मा

कलाश्चैता अधमस्येह पुंस:॥

“The following are the vilest of persons: An unjust and cruel king; a person full of several shortcomings; a tainted person; someone who out of anger denounces everyone else; an ungrateful person; a person not friendly with anyone else and one who is evil at heart. (18)

(श्लोक-19)

न श्रद्दधाति कल्याणं परेभ्योऽप्यात्मशंकित:।

निराकरोति मित्राणि यो वै सोऽधमपूरुष:॥

“He is mean who out of self-doubt does not believe in doing well to others. He keeps a distance even from his friends. (19)

(श्लोक-20)

उत्तमानेव सेवेत प्राप्तकाले तु मध्यमान्।

अधमांस्तु न सेवेत य इच्छेद् भूतिमात्मन:॥

“For your uplift serve or seek help from the most superior persons. If unavoidable, go to persons at the next level. However, under no circumstances, cringe in front of the vilest to ask for a gain. (20)

(श्लोक-21)

प्राप्नोति वै वित्तमसद्बलेन

नित्योत्थानात् प्रज्ञया पौरुषेण।

न त्वेव सम्यग् लभते प्रशंसां

न वृत्तमाप्नोति महाकुलानाम्॥

“Even if a man gets enriched with help from undesirable persons, or by putting in tireless effort, because of his wisdom or by sheer hard work, he cannot hope to attain the respect, character and dignity of the persons of reputed families.” (21)

धृतराष्ट्र उवाच

(श्लोक-22)

महाकुलेभ्य: स्पृहयन्ति देवा

धर्मार्थनित्याश्च बहुश्रुताश्च।

पृच्छामि त्वां विदुर प्रश्नमेतं

भवन्ति वै कानि महाकुलानि॥

At this stage, Dh¾tarŒ¦¢ra intervened and said: “Vidura! Even the devatŒs, knowledgeable in Dharma and Artha, desire to be friends with the persons from families of status. Please tell me which these superior families are?” (22)

विदुर उवाच

(श्लोक-23)

तपो दमो ब्रह्मवित्तं विताना:

पुण्या विवाहा: सततान्नदानम्।

येष्वेवैते सप्त गुणा वसन्ति

सम्यग्वृत्तास्तानि महाकुलानि॥

Vidura replied: “Those families are superior to others, the members of which have the following attributes: Meditation or penance; subjugation of senses; a study of the Vedas; performance of yajÛa; holy matrimonial alliances; distribution of grains in charity all the time and noble conduct. (23)

(श्लोक-24)

येषां हि वृत्तं व्यथते न योनि-

श्चित्तप्रसादेन चरन्ति धर्मम्।

ते कीर्तिमिच्छन्ति कुले विशिष्टां

त्यक्तानृतास्तानि महाकुलानि॥

“The families of repute are never lax in their moral attitudes. Their young members do not cause pain to their parents with their shortcomings. Such families willingly follow the path of Dharma. By rejecting the path of falsehood, these families enhance their prestige. (24)

(श्लोक-25)

अनिज्यया कुविवाहैर्वेदस्योत्सादनेन च।

कुलान्यकुलतां यान्ति धर्मस्यातिक्रमेण च॥

“The families decline if they do not perform YajÛa, if they marry in tainted families, if they give up reading the scriptures or if they violate the tenets of Dharma. (25)

(श्लोक-26)

देवद्रव्यविनाशेन ब्रह्मस्वहरणेन च।

कुलान्यकुलतां यान्ति ब्राह्मणातिक्रमेण च॥

“Destroying the wealth of the DevatŒs, usurping a Brahmin’s assets and overstepping the limit of decorum and decency in dealing with the Brahmins, even the highly regarded families come to naught. (26)

(श्लोक-27)

ब्राह्मणानां परिभवात् परिवादाच्च भारत।

कुलान्यकुलतां यान्ति न्यासापहरणेन च॥

“Bharata, humiliating the Brahmins or usurping the pledged wealth bring disrepute even to families of status. (27)

(श्लोक-28)

कुलानि समुपेतानि गोभि: पुरुषतोऽर्थत:।

कुलसंख्यां न गच्छन्ति यानि हीनानि वृत्तत:॥

“The families which, even though endowed with the wealth of cows, human beings or riches, do not maintain a high moral character, cannot be counted among the nobility. (28)

(श्लोक-29)

वृत्ततस्त्वविहीनानि कुलान्यल्पधनान्यपि।

कुलसंख्यां च गच्छन्ति कर्षन्ति च महद् यश:॥

“The families of high moral conduct even though poor, can be counted among the superiors. Their prestige is enhanced by their conduct. (29)

(श्लोक-30)

वृत्तं यत्नेन संरक्षेद् वित्तमेति च याति च।

अक्षीणो वित्तत: क्षीणो वृत्ततस्तु हतो हत:॥

“Take care that you do not deviate from the path of the righteous conduct. Wealth is transitory. It comes and goes. A virtuous man, even though reduced to penury, is not considered a pauper. However, the one who has strayed from the path of virtue, is diminished for good. (30)

(श्लोक-31)

गोभि: पशुभिरश्वैश्च कृष्या च सुसमृद्धया।

कुलानि न प्ररोहन्ति यानि हीनानि वृत्तत:॥

“Families devoid of virtue cannot prosper even if they are rich in milch cattle, horses, other animals and fields rich in harvest. (31)

(श्लोक-32)

मा न: कुले वैरकृत् कश्चिदस्तु

राजामात्यो मा परस्वापहारी।

मित्रद्रोही नैकृतिकोऽनृती वा

पूर्वाशी वा पितृदेवातिथिभ्य:॥

“In our families, may there be none who is inimical to others! May there be no king or minister who usurps the wealth belonging to others! May none be a traitor, a cheat, or the one indulging in falsehood! May there be none who takes a meal before offering it to the ancestors, the parents and the guests. (32)

(श्लोक-33)

यश्च नो ब्राह्मणान् हन्याद्यश्च नो ब्राह्मणान् द्विषेत् ।

न न: स समितिं गच्छेद्यश्च नो निर्वपेत् पितॄन्॥

“He shall not enter our assembly who kills a Brahmin, who is jealous of a Brahmin, and who does not offer PiÄÎa [a round mass or ball of food] and TarpaÄa [presenting libations of water] to the ancestors. (33)

(श्लोक-34)

तृणानि भूमिरुदकं वाक् चतुर्थी च सूनृता।

सतामेतानि गेहेषु नोच्छिद्यन्ते कदाचन॥३४॥

“A virtuous person is never in short supply of a mat of grass, earth to sit upon, water and sweet words. (34)

(श्लोक-35)

श्रद्धया परया राजन्नुपनीतानि सत्कृतिम्।

प्रवृत्तानि महाप्राज्ञ धर्मिणां पुण्यकर्मिणाम्॥

“My wise King! These four items are faithfully and devotedly offered to welcome the guests in the homes of the virtuous who are otherwise busy in holy pursuits. (35)

(श्लोक-36)

सूक्ष्मोऽपि भारं नृपते स्यन्दनो वै

शक्तो वोढुं न तथान्ये महीजा:।

एवं युक्ता भारसहा भवन्ति

महाकुलीना न तथान्ये मनुष्या:॥

“Even a small carriage can carry a heavy load while a large piece of wood cannot. Likewise, only the courageous persons of high descent can carry heavy responsibility and not others. (36)

(श्लोक-37)

न तन्मित्रं यस्य कोपाद् बिभेति

यद् वा मित्रं शंकितेनोपचर्यम्।

यस्मिन् मित्रे पितरीवाश्वसीत

तद् वै मित्रं संगतानीतराणि॥

“He is not a friend who always inspires fear with his angry behaviour or who has been placated for fear of harm. He alone is a true friend who can be trusted like a father. Others are at best companions. (37)

(श्लोक-38)

य: कश्चिदप्यसम्बद्धो मित्रभावेन वर्तते।

स एव बन्धुस्तन्मित्रं सा गतिस्तत् परायणम्॥

“Even a hitherto stranger, if he behaves like a friend, becomes a relation, a friend, support and shelter. (38)

(श्लोक-39)

चलचित्तस्य वै पुंसो वृद्धाननुपसेवत:।

पारिप्लवमतेर्नित्यमध्रुवो मित्रसंग्रह:॥

“A fickle person, or one who does not look after the elderly, or one whose thinking is never stable, can never make permanent friends. (39)

(श्लोक-40)

चलचित्तमनात्मानमिन्द्रियाणां वशानुगम्।

अर्था: समभिवर्तन्ते हंसा: शुष्कं सरो यथा॥

“An ignorant man, a man who changes his mind all the time, or a man who is ensnared by his lustful senses, cannot attain Artha [objective, desire, riches]. He is like a HaÅsa which hovers around a lake that has dried but never steps into it. (40)

(श्लोक-41)

अकस्मादेव कुप्यन्ति प्रसीदन्त्यनिमित्तत:।

शीलमेतदसाधूनामभ्रं पारिप्लवं यथा॥

“An evil man’s temperament is fickle like that of a cloud. He gets angry for no reason and is pleased without justification. (41)

(श्लोक-42)

सत्कृताश्च कृतार्थाश्च मित्राणां न भवन्ति ये।

तान् मृतानपि क्रव्यादा: कृतघ्नान्नोपभुञ्जते॥

“Even carnivores do not feed upon the flesh of ungrateful men who betray their friends who have helped them and who have treated them with kindness. (42)

(श्लोक-43)

अर्चयेदेव मित्राणि सति वासति वा धने।

नानर्थयन् प्रजानाति मित्राणां सारफल्गुताम्॥

“Honour a friend whether he is rich or a pauper. Making no demand on friends, one ought not to look forward to material benefits from them. (43)

(श्लोक-44)

संतापाद् भ्रश्यते रूपं संतापाद् भ्रश्यते बलम्।

संतापाद् भ्रश्यते ज्ञानं संतापाद् व्याधिमृच्छति॥

“Distress, grief, pessimism or worry destroy beauty, valour, and knowledge. Rather, a person affected by these negative attitudes, soon becomes sick. (44)

(श्लोक-45)

अनवाप्यं च शोकेन शरीरं चोपतप्यते।

अमित्राश्च प्रहृष्यन्ति मा स्म शोके मन: कृथा:॥

“Do not grieve because it does not beget what you aspire to. Grief merely brings bodily suffering and pleases your enemies. (45)

(श्लोक-46)

पुनर्नरो म्रियते जायते च

पुनर्नरो हीयते वर्धते च।

पुनर्नरो याचति याच्यते च

पुनर्नर: शोचति शोच्यते च॥

“A human being dies repeatedly and is reborn. He suffers losses and recoups them. He asks others for favours while at another time others approach him for their benefit. Similarly, in life he grieves for others while at another time they grieve for him. (46)

(श्लोक-47)

सुखं च दु:खं च भवाभवौ च

लाभालाभौ मरणं जीवितं च।

पर्यायश: सर्वमेते स्पृशन्ति

तस्माद् धीरो न च हृष्येन्न शोचेत्॥

“Joy and sorrow, creation and destruction, profit or loss, birth or death, are a part of every life cycle. Hence a composed person should neither rejoice in nor moan their occurrence. (47)

(श्लोक-48)

चलानि हीमानि षडिन्द्रियाणि

तेषां यद् यद् वर्धते यत्र यत्र।

ततस्तत: स्रवते बुद्धिरस्य

छिद्रोदकुम्भादिव नित्यमम्भ:॥

“Our six senses are quite fickle. When these perceptions [recklessly] indulge in a certain deed, discretion is correspondingly extinguished. It is like water leaking out of a pot with a hole.” (48)

धृतराष्ट्र उवाच

(श्लोक-49)

तनुरुद्ध: शिखी राजा मिथ्योपचरितो मया।

मन्दानां मम पुत्राणां युद्धेनान्तं करिष्यति॥

Dh¾tarŒ¦¢ra interrupted: “I have treated Yudhi¦¢hira deviously. He is bound by the essence of Dharma like fire hidden in a piece of wood. The PŒÄÎavas will now wage a war and destroy my foolish progeny. (49)

(श्लोक-50)

नित्योद्विग्नमिदं सर्वं नित्योद्विग्नमिदं मन:।

यत् तत् पदमनुद्विग्नं तन्मे वद महामते॥

“O wise brother! This fear agitates my mind. Everything around me too is upset by the thought of an impending catastrophe. Therefore, guide me to the path of peace. Advise me how to quench my apprehension.” (50)

विदुर उवाच

(श्लोक-51)

नान्यत्र विद्यातपसोर्नान्यत्रेन्द्रियनिग्रहात्।

नान्यत्र लोभसंत्यागाच्छान्तिं पश्यामि तेऽनघ॥

Vidura replied: “My faultless king! I do not see any other means of pacifying your apprehensions except erudition, TapasyŒ, abjuration of greed and command of the five senses. (51)

“Be kind to an employee and treat him as an equal if he understands the nuances of your thinking on various issues. “An employee who disobeys his master’s command, who declines to carry out an assignment, who overestimates his intelligence, or who contradicts his employer, ought to be dismissed at the earliest. (26)

(श्लोक-27)

“A man is suitable to be a messenger or an ambassador if he has the following eight attributes:

“A smart person does not, in misplaced faith, untimely visit the place of an undependable man. He does not stand in hiding at crossroads at night. He never tries to win the affection of a woman in whom the king is interested. (28)

“A smart courtier will not contradict the king in an assembly where the latter is consulting vicious advisers in a group. He must not utter the words: ‘I do not believe it.’ He ought to remove himself from such a gathering on some pretext or the other. (29)

“A smart person avoids dealing with a king who is unusually kind and generous, with a woman of disrepute, government servants, a son, a brother, a widow with young children, soldiers or with men deprived of their rights. (30)

(श्लोक-31)

अष्टौ गुणा: पुरुषं दीपयन्ति

प्रज्ञा च कौल्यं च श्रुतं दमश्च।

“The following eight virtues add to a man’s prestige:

एतान् गुणांस्तात महानुभावा-

“Brother! There is another virtue which powerfully overrides the virtues listed above. When the king welcomes a person, this particular quality stands out. (32)

गुणा दश स्नानशीलं भजन्ते

“The person who takes a daily bath gets the following ten rewards:

“A person who is restrained in food intake gets the following six rewards:

“Do not give shelter to the following: An idle person; a glutton; one who is inimical to everyone; a trickster; a cruel person; a man who ignores the limitations of time and place and a person dressed unsuitably. (35)

पश्य दोषान् पाण्डवैर्विग्रहे त्वं

(श्लोक-6)

क्षयोऽपि बहु मन्तव्यो य: क्षयो वृद्धिमावहेत्॥

“An accretion that forebades ill for the future should not be taken seriously. On the contrary, even a depletion is welcome if it augurs well for the future. (6)

(श्लोक-7)

न स क्षयो महाराज य: क्षयो वृद्धिमावहेत्।

क्षय: स त्विह मन्तव्यो यं लब्ध्वा बहु नाशयेत्॥

“Maharaj! A decline which rebounds into an ascent, is not in reality a setback. However, any current advantage is not welcome if it converts into a set back and destruction later on. (7)

(श्लोक-8)

समृद्धा गुणत: केचिद् भवन्ति धनतोऽपरे।

धनवृद्धान् गुणैर्हीनान् धृतराष्ट्र विवर्जय॥

“Dh¾tarŒ¦¢ra! Some are rich in virtues while others are wealthy. However, if a rich person lacks virtues, he ought to be shunned completely.” (8)

धृतराष्ट्र उवाच

(श्लोक-9)

सर्वं त्वमायतीयुक्तं भाषसे प्राज्ञसम्मतम्।

न चोत्सहे सुतं त्यक्तुं यतो धर्मस्ततो जय:॥

Dh¾tarŒ¦¢ra interrupted and said, “The end result of whatever you say is beneficial. The wise endorse it. It is also true that Dharma alone is the victor. In spite of this, I am unable to desert my son.” (9)

विदुर उवाच

(श्लोक-10)

अतीवगुणसम्पन्नो न जातु विनयान्वित:।

सुसूक्ष्ममपि भूतानामुपमर्दमुपेक्षते॥

Vidura responded: “A deeply virtuous and humble man cannot withstand the shightest annihilation of mankind nor turn his face away. (10)

(श्लोक-11-12)

परापवादनिरता: परदु:खोदयेषु च।

परस्परविरोधे च यतन्ते सततोत्थिता:॥

सदोषं दर्शनं येषां संवासे सुमहद् भयम्।

अर्थादाने महान् दोष: प्रदाने च महद् भयम्॥

“It is inadvisable to borrow money from those who denounce others all the time, who enjoy causing pain to others, who relish destroying the unity among others, whose thinking is perverse and whose association can be highly dangerous. There is danger in lending them either. (11-12)

(श्लोक-13)

ये वै भेदनशीलास्तु सकामा निस्त्रपा: शठा:।

ये पापा इति विख्याता: संवासे परिगर्हिता:॥

“Avoid the company of those who sow seeds of dissension, who are lechers, shameless, vicious and well-known rascals. Such persons are condemnable. (13)

(श्लोक-14)

युक्तांश्चान्यैर्महादोषैर्ये नरास्तान् विवर्जयेत्।

निवर्तमाने सौहार्दे प्रीतिर्नीचे प्रणश्यति॥

या चैव फलनिर्वृत्ति: सौहृदे चैव यत् सुखम्।

“Shun those as well who have other shortcomings besides the ones mentioned above. The mean persons stop being affectionate once their friendship is over. The joys and any other advantages accruing from such friendship are also lost. (14 1/2)

(श्लोक-15)

यतते चापवादाय यत्नमारभते क्षये॥

अल्पेऽप्यपकृते मोहान्न शान्तिमधिगच्छति।

“Thereafter, that mean person denounces his former friends. He tries to harm them if they are even slightly at fault. He is never at peace. (15 1/2)

(श्लोक-16)

तादृशै: संगतं नीचैर्नृशंसैरकृतात्मभि:॥

निशम्य निपुणं बुद्धॺा विद्वान् दूराद् विवर्जयेत्।

“Considering the damage that is likely to be caused to himself by an association with such mean, cruel, and lecherous persons, the learned man must eschew any contact with them. (16 1/2)

(श्लोक-17)

यो ज्ञातिमनुगृह्णाति दरिद्रं दीनमातुरम्॥

स पुत्रपशुभिर्वृद्धिंश्रेयश्चानन्त्यमश्नुते।

“A man who obliges his kith and kin, the poor, the humble and the sick, prospers in sons and animals. He enjoys immense prosperity. (17 1/2)

(श्लोक-18)

ज्ञातयो वर्धनीयास्तैर्य इच्छन्त्यात्मन: शुभम्॥

कुलवृद्धिं च राजेन्द्र तस्मात् साधु समाचर।

“RŒjendra! Those who desire their own welfare, should help their near and dear ones to progress. Therefore, go ahead and add to your clan. (18 1/2)

(श्लोक-19)

श्रेयसा योक्ष्यते राजन् कुर्वाणो ज्ञातिसत्क्रियाम्॥

“RŒjan! The one who treats the members of his family with respect, deserves to be prosperous. (19)

(श्लोक-20)

विगुणा ह्यपि संरक्ष्या ज्ञातयो भरतर्षभ।

किं पुनर्गुणवन्तस्ते त्वत्प्रसादाभिकाङ्क्षिण:॥

“O superior among the BhŒratas! Even if the members of your family are devoid of virtue, they ought to be protected. Naturally, your virtuous well-wishers deserve a higher consideration. (20)

(श्लोक-21)

प्रसादं कुरु वीराणां पाण्डवानां विशाम्पते।

दीयन्तां ग्रामका: केचित् तेषां वृत्त्यर्थमीश्वर॥

“RŒjan! You are competent to oblige the chivalrous PŒÄÎavas. Hand them over a few villages for their upkeep. (21)

(श्लोक-22)

एवं लोके यश: प्राप्तं भविष्यति नराधिप।

वृद्धेन हि त्वया कार्यं पुत्राणां तात शासनम्॥

“NareÜwara! You will earn a good name in the world if you do so. Brother! You are ageing. You ought to exercise control over your sons. (22)

(श्लोक-23)

मया चापि हितं वाच्यं विद्धि मां त्वद्धितैषिणम्।

ज्ञातिभिर्विग्रहस्तात न कर्तव्य: शुभार्थिना।

सुखानि सह भोज्यानि ज्ञातिभिर्भरतर्षभ॥

“BharataÜre¦¢ha! Please think of me as your well-wisher. I speak for your benefit. Brother, if a person desires everyone’s well-being, he must not quarrel with his relatives. Rather, he should share his happiness with them. (23)

(श्लोक-24)

सम्भोजनं संकथनं सम्प्रीतिश्च परस्परम्।

ज्ञातिभि: सह कार्याणि न विरोध: कदाचन॥

“Members of a clan ought to share a meal, pleasant conversation and affection. Such is the duty of each one of them. They must avoid conflict. (24)

(श्लोक-25)

ज्ञातयस्तारयन्तीह ज्ञातयो मज्जयन्ति च।

सुवृत्तास्तारयन्तीह दुर्वृत्ता मज्जयन्ति च॥

“In this world your kith and kin help you to sink or swim. The good ones among them help you to tide over the flood. The vicious push you down to drown. (25)

(श्लोक-26)

सुवृत्तो भव राजेन्द्र पाण्डवान् प्रति मानद।

अधर्षणीय: शत्रूणां तैर्वृतस्त्वं भविष्यसि॥

“RŒjendra! Treat the PŒÄÎavas fairly. O Respectful ! protected by them you will be safe from your enemies, if any. (26)

(श्लोक-27)

श्रीमन्तं ज्ञातिमासाद्य यो ज्ञातिरवसीदति।

दिग्धहस्तं मृग इव स एनस्तस्य विन्दति॥

“A deer suffers when it nears a hunter carrying poisonous arrows. If a relative suffers when in the vicinity of a rich man, it is the latter who suffers the consequences of the sin [of not helping him in time of need.] (27)

(श्लोक-28)

पश्चादपि नरश्रेष्ठ तव तापो भविष्यति।

तान् वा हतान् सुतान् वापि श्रुत्वा तदनुचिन्तय॥

“NaraÜre¦¢ha! Later on you will repent over the death of the PŒÄÎavas or of your sons. Just think over the situation. [There is still time.] (28)

(श्लोक-29)

येन खट्वां समारूढ: परितप्येत कर्मणा।

आदावेव न तत् कुर्यादध्रुवे जीविते सति॥

“Life is not certain. Do not commit an act if you have to repent at its consequences later on.” (29)

(श्लोक-30)

न कश्चिन्नापनयते पुमानन्यत्र भार्गवात्।

शेषसम्प्रतिपत्तिस्तु बुद्धिमत्स्वेव तिष्ठति॥

“Barring ÝukrŒcŒrya, there is none who has not violated ethics at one time or the other. Let bygones be bygone. It is for a wise person like you to carefully decide the future course of action. (30)

(श्लोक-31)

दुर्योधनेन यद्येतत् पापं तेषु पुरा कृतम्।

त्वया तत् कुलवृद्धेन प्रत्यानेयं नरेश्वर॥

“NareÜwara! If Duryodhana has earlier caused harm to PŒÄÎavas, as the eldest in the family it is your duty to compensate them. (31)

(श्लोक-32)

तांस्त्वं पदे प्रतिष्ठाप्य लोके विगतकल्मष:।

भविष्यसि नरश्रेष्ठ पूजनीयो मनीषिणाम्॥

“NaraÜre¦¢ha! If you hand over the kingdom to the PŒÄÎavas, it will wipe out the blot on your name. The wise will regard you with respect. (32)

(श्लोक-33)

सुव्याहृतानि धीराणां फलत: परिचिन्त्य य:।

अध्यवस्यति कार्येषु चिरं यशसि तिष्ठति॥

“The man who paying attention to a wise man’s words, acts upon them, is remembered for a long time. (33)

(श्लोक-34)

असम्यगुपयुक्तं हि ज्ञानं सुकुशलैरपि।

उपलभ्यं चाविदितं विदितं चाननुष्ठितम्॥

“The advice tendered by the learned goes waste if it does not enlighten the listener of his duty or if not acted upon. (34)

(श्लोक-35)

पापोदयफलं विद्वान् यो नारभति वर्धते।

यस्तु पूर्वकृतं पापमविमृश्यानुवर्तते।

अगाधपङ्के दुर्मेधा विषमे विनिपात्यते॥

“A learned man prospers if he does not initiate an evil task. On the contrary, the one with a perverse vision who continues following the evil course of actions undertaken earlier, is pushed into a limitless mire in the hell. (35)

(श्लोक-36)

मन्त्रभेदस्य षट् प्राज्ञो द्वाराणीमानि लक्षयेत्।

अर्थसंततिकामश्च रक्षेदेतानि नित्यश:॥

मदं स्वप्नमविज्ञानमाकारं चात्मसम्भवम्।

दुष्टामात्येषु विश्रम्भं दूताच्चाकुशलादपि॥

“A wise man need understand the following six leakages of confidential discussions of wealth and plug the same. This will help protect his riches:

1. Drinking habit, 2. Sleep, 3. Ignorance of the essentials, 4. Maladies of the eyes and of the mouth, 5. Blind faith in dishonest ministers, 6. Trust in a foolish messenger. (36-37)

(श्लोक-38)

द्वाराण्येतानि यो ज्ञात्वा संवृणोति सदा नृप।

त्रिवर्गाचरणे युक्त: स शत्रूनधितिष्ठति॥

“RŒjan! The one who always keeps these leaking exits shut and is occupied in the pursuit of Dharma, Artha and KŒma, gains control of his enemies. (38)

(श्लोक-39)

न वै श्रुतमविज्ञाय वृद्धाननुपसेव्य वा।

धर्मार्थौ वेदितुं शक्यौ बृहस्पतिसमैरपि॥

“Even like B¾haspati, a man cannot, without the knowledge of the scriptures or without looking after his elders, understand the significance of Dharma and Artha. (39)

(श्लोक-40)

नष्टं समुद्रे पतितं नष्टं वाक्यमशृण्वति।

अनात्मनि श्रुतं नष्टं नष्टं हुतमनग्निकम्॥

“An object falling into the ocean drowns and is destroyed. An advice is lost upon a person who does not pay heed. The learning of a person is lost who has no control over his senses, and Havana made in ashes is useless similarly. (40)

(श्लोक-41)

मत्या परीक्ष्य मेधावी बुद्धॺा सम्पाद्य चासकृत्।

श्रुत्वा दृष्ट्वाथ विज्ञाय प्राज्ञैर्मैत्रीं समाचरेत्॥

“Even the learned ought to be judged wisely, with discretion and by yardsticks born of experience, before making them friends. Suitable and discreet investigation need to be carried out. Others too can be consulted for advice in the matter. (41)

(श्लोक-42)

अकीर्तिं विनयो हन्ति हन्त्यनर्थं पराक्रम:।

हन्ति नित्यं क्षमा क्रोधमाचारो हन्त्यलक्षणम्॥

“Humility makes up for lack of renown, Valour defeats misfortune, Forgiveness wipes out anger, Good conduct overcomes many a shortcoming. (42)

(श्लोक-43)

परिच्छदेन क्षेत्रेण वेश्मना परिचर्यया।

परीक्षेत कुलं राजन् भोजनाच्छादनेन च॥

“RŒjan! A family should be judged by the following yardsticks: Availability of varied objects of luxury; the mother-side relatives the house itself; the manners of receiving the guests; quality of food served and the type of clothing used. (43)

(श्लोक-44)

उपस्थितस्य कामस्य प्रतिवादो न विद्यते।

अपि निर्मुक्तदेहस्य कामरक्तस्य किं पुन:॥

“Even a man devoid of arrogance about his body does not spurn a justified offering, though he had never asked for it. How then can a lecher give it up? (44)

(श्लोक-45)

प्राज्ञोपसेविनं वैद्यं धार्मिकं प्रियदर्शनम्।

मित्रवन्तं सुवाक्यं च सुहृदं परिपालयेत्॥

“Protect a friend who is a Vaidya, who serves the learned, who is religious, handsome, and who has several friends, and is polite of speech. (45)

(श्लोक-46)

दुष्कुलीन: कुलीनो वा मर्यादां यो न लंघयेत् ।

धर्मापेक्षी मृदुर्ह्रीमान् स कुलीनशताद् वर:॥

“Whether born low or high, a man who does not violate the principles of Dharma, stays within the limits of morality, is mild tempered and restrained, is superior to hundreds of the high-born. (46)

(श्लोक-47)

ययोश्चित्तेन वा चित्तं निभृतं निभृतेन वा।

समेति प्रज्ञया प्रज्ञा तयोर्मैत्री न जीर्यति॥

“It makes for a durable friendship if two persons think alike [under different situations], share and keep secrets and are of the same views [in matters where an application of intelligence is called for]. (47)

(श्लोक-48)

दुर्बुद्धिमकृतप्रज्ञं छन्नं कूपं तृणैरिव।

विवर्जयीत मेधावी तस्मिन् मैत्री प्रणश्यति॥

“Like avoiding a well, covered with grass and straws, a wise man must give up his association with a vicious person who is devoid of clear thinking. Such a friendship wanes in any case. (48)

(श्लोक-47)

अवलिप्तेषु मूर्खेषु रौद्रसाहसिकेषु च।

तथैवापेतधर्मेषु न मैत्रीमाचरेद् बुध:॥

“A wise person must not befriend an arrogant person, a fool, an angry man, a thoughtlessly courageous or an irreligious person. (49)

(श्लोक-50)

कृतज्ञं धार्मिकं सत्यमक्षुद्रं दृढभक्तिकम्।

जितेन्द्रियं स्थितं स्थित्यां मित्रमत्यागि चेष्यते॥

“A friend ought to have the following traits:

1. Gratitude, 2. Religiousity, 3. Truthfulness, 4. Generosity, 5. Firm devotion, 6. Conquest of senses, 7. Appreciation of his limitations, 8. Firm friendship. (50)

(श्लोक-51)

इन्द्रियाणामनुत्सर्गो मृत्युनापि विशिष्यते।

अत्यर्थं पुनरुत्सर्ग: सादयेद् दैवतान्यपि॥

“A complete subordination of the senses is as difficult as an effort to avoid death. However, allowing them complete freedom can destroy even the divinity of DevatŒs. (51)

(श्लोक-52)

मार्दवं सर्वभूतानामनसूया क्षमा धृति:।

आयुष्याणि बुधा: प्राहुर्मित्राणां चाविमानना॥

“According to the wise, the following virtues add to longevity:

1. Kindness to all creatures, 2. Not finding fault with the virtues of others, 3. Forgiveness, 4. Composure, 5. Never insulting a friend. (52)

(श्लोक-53)

अपनीतं सुनीतेन योऽर्थं प्रत्यानिनीषते।

मतिमास्थाय सुदृढां तदकापुरुषव्रतम्॥

“Anyone who desires to regain the wealth unjustly destroyed, by taking recourse to unshakable wisdom, with noble intentions, acts like a brave man. (53)

(श्लोक-54)

आयत्यां प्रतिकारज्ञस्तदात्वे दृढनिश्चय:।

अतीते कार्यशेषज्ञो नरोऽर्थैर्न प्रहीयते॥

“A man who knows how to prevent an impending calamity, who is firmly determined to do his duty in the present, and who is aware of the unfinished tasks in the past, is never reduced to penury. (54)

(श्लोक-55)

कर्मणा मनसा वाचा यदभीक्ष्णं निषेवते।

तदेवापहरत्येनं तस्मात् कल्याणमाचरेत्॥

“A noble deed done wholeheartedly and steadily, in speech, thought and action, is fascinating and attractive. Therefore, always act for the welfare of others and perform good deeds. (55)

(श्लोक-56)

मङ्गलालम्भनं योग: श्रुतमुत्थानमार्जवम्।

भूतिमेतानि कुर्वन्ति सतां चाभीक्ष्णदर्शनम्॥

“The following activities are beneficial: Contact with auspicious objects; control of the wayward mind; a study of the scriptures; hard work; simplicity and frequent contact with truthful and noble persons. (56)

(श्लोक-57)

अनिर्वेद: श्रियो मूलं लाभस्य च शुभस्य च।

महान् भवत्यनिर्विण्ण: सुखं चानन्त्यमश्नुते॥

“To work consistently, without interruption, is a source of money, profit and welfare. That is why a hardworking man rises to great heights and lives happily. (57)

(श्लोक-58)

नात: श्रीमत्तरं किञ्चिदन्यत् पथ्यतमं मतम्।

प्रभविष्णोर्यथा तात क्षमा सर्वत्र सर्वदा॥

“Brother! For a well-endowed man, there is no other measure to bless him with more wealth and beneficial in all respects, than forgiveness at all times and place. (58)

(श्लोक-59)
