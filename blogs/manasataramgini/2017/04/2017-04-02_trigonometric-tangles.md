Let us define a define the trigonometric tangle as the following
parametric function:  
![x=\\cos(t/k)-a\\cos(b\\cdot
t)\\sin(t)](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=x%3D%5Ccos%28t%2Fk%29-a%5Ccos%28b%5Ccdot+t%29%5Csin%28t%29&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"x=\\cos(t/k)-a\\cos(b\\cdot t)\\sin(t)")  
![y=\\sin(t/k)-a\\cos(b\\cdot
t)\\cos(t)](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=y%3D%5Csin%28t%2Fk%29-a%5Ccos%28b%5Ccdot+t%29%5Ccos%28t%29&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"y=\\sin(t/k)-a\\cos(b\\cdot t)\\cos(t)")  
where ![k](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=k&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"k") can be a rational number
![\\frac{p}{q}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Cfrac%7Bp%7D%7Bq%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\frac{p}{q}") or an irrational number.
![a](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=a&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "a")
and ![b](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=b&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"b") are any real number. If
![k](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=k&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "k") is
a rational number and
![|b|=1](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7Cb%7C%3D1&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"|b|=1") then we get a tangle
![c](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=c&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "c")
petals defined thus:  
![k=\\dfrac{p}{q}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=k%3D%5Cdfrac%7Bp%7D%7Bq%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"k=\\dfrac{p}{q}")

![\\dfrac{c}{d}=\\dfrac{2p+q}{q}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Cdfrac%7Bc%7D%7Bd%7D%3D%5Cdfrac%7B2p%2Bq%7D%7Bq%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\dfrac{c}{d}=\\dfrac{2p+q}{q}"), such that
![c,d](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=c%2Cd&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"c,d") are relatively prime.

When ![|b|\\gg
1](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7Cb%7C%5Cgg+1&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"|b|\\gg 1") then the envelop of the tangle converges to a figure with
![e](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=e&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"e")-fold symmetry defined thus:  
![\\dfrac{e}{f}=\\dfrac{2p+2q}{q}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Cdfrac%7Be%7D%7Bf%7D%3D%5Cdfrac%7B2p%2B2q%7D%7Bq%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\dfrac{e}{f}=\\dfrac{2p+2q}{q}") such that
![e,f](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=e%2Cf&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"e,f") are relatively prime.

For irrational
![k](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=k&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "k")
for the above conditions we get figures coming close those of
![k=\\frac{p}{q}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=k%3D%5Cfrac%7Bp%7D%7Bq%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"k=\\frac{p}{q}") corresponding to the continued fraction approximations
of the irrationals. This provides us an interesting way of visualizing
the irrationals. Interestingly, visual distinction can be made between
algebraic and transcendental irrationals with these curves.
