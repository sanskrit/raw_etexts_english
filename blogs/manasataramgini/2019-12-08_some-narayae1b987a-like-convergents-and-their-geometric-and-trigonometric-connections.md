While playing with an iterative geometric construction in our youth we
discovered for ourselves a particular right triangle whose sides are in
the proportion ![1: \\sqrt{\\phi} :
\\phi](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=1%3A+%5Csqrt%7B%5Cphi%7D+%3A+%5Cphi&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"1: \\sqrt{\\phi} : \\phi"), where ![\\phi=
\\tfrac{1+\\sqrt{5}}{2}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Cphi%3D+%5Ctfrac%7B1%2B%5Csqrt%7B5%7D%7D%7B2%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\phi= \\tfrac{1+\\sqrt{5}}{2}") is the Golden Ratio. This triangle is
of course famous as being the basis of the largest triangles of the
Śrīyantra and the Tripuraśekhara- (Bālā-) yantra of the [Śrīkula
tradition](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2019/10/06/the-minimal-triangle-circumscribing-a-semicircle/)
and also the famous [Great
Pyramid](https://manasataramgini.wordpress.com/2016/08/23/some-meanderings-among-golden-stuff/)
of the ancient Egyptians. Revisiting it, we were able to provide the
trigonometric proof for our construction, which in turn led several
sequences of interest. The construction goes thus (Figure 1):

*[![root\_Golden\_convergence](https://manasataramgini.files.wordpress.com/2019/12/root_golden_convergence.png?w=640)](https://manasataramgini.files.wordpress.com/2019/12/root_golden_convergence.png)Figure
1*

1\) Start with an isosceles right triangle ![\\triangle
AOB](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Ctriangle+AOB&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\triangle AOB") or ![\\triangle
A'OB](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Ctriangle+A%27OB&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\triangle A'OB"), whose equal sides are 1 each and hypotenuse is
![\\sqrt{2}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Csqrt%7B2%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\sqrt{2}").  
2\) Slide the hypotenuse down to point
![O](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=O&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "O")
create a segment equal to it in length
![\\overline{OC\_1}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BOC_1%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{OC_1}"). Join point
![C\_1](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=C_1&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"C_1") to
![B](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=B&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "B").  
3\) Draw a perpendicular to
![\\overline{C\_1B}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BC_1B%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{C_1B}") to meet the base line
![\\overleftrightarrow{AC\_1}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverleftrightarrow%7BAC_1%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overleftrightarrow{AC_1}") to meet it at
![C\_1'](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=C_1%27&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"C_1'"). Joint
![C\_1'](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=C_1%27&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"C_1'") to
![B](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=B&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "B") to
create a right triangle ![\\triangle
C\_1'OB](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Ctriangle+C_1%27OB&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\triangle C_1'OB").  
4\) Draw a segment equal to the hypotenuse
![\\overline{C\_1'B}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BC_1%27B%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{C_1'B}") of this new triangle along the baseline
![\\overleftrightarrow{AC\_1}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverleftrightarrow%7BAC_1%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overleftrightarrow{AC_1}") to get a point at the distance equal to
![\\overline{C\_1'B}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BC_1%27B%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{C_1'B}") from
![O](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=O&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0 "O").  
5\) Iterate the above steps with this point.

What will happen if you keep iterating thus? We learned to our surprise
in our youth that it converges to a right triangle ![\\triangle
K\_1BK\_2](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Ctriangle+K_1BK_2&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\triangle K_1BK_2") (shaded yellow in Figure 1), whose sides will be
![(\\sqrt{\\phi}, \\phi,
\\phi^{3/2})](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%28%5Csqrt%7B%5Cphi%7D%2C+%5Cphi%2C+%5Cphi%5E%7B3%2F2%7D%29&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"(\\sqrt{\\phi}, \\phi, \\phi^{3/2})"). Further the altitude
![\\overline{OB}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BOB%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{OB}") will divide the hypotenuse into segments
![\\overline{OK\_1}=\\sqrt{\\tfrac{1}{\\phi}}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BOK_1%7D%3D%5Csqrt%7B%5Ctfrac%7B1%7D%7B%5Cphi%7D%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{OK_1}=\\sqrt{\\tfrac{1}{\\phi}}") and
![\\overline{OK\_1}=\\sqrt{\\phi}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BOK_1%7D%3D%5Csqrt%7B%5Cphi%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{OK_1}=\\sqrt{\\phi}").

The trigonometric proof we obtained for this is rather simple. It
essential comprises of finding the attractor of the following map:

![x\_{n+1}=\\cos\\left(\\arctan(x\_n)\\right)](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=x_%7Bn%2B1%7D%3D%5Ccos%5Cleft%28%5Carctan%28x_n%29%5Cright%29&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"x_{n+1}=\\cos\\left(\\arctan(x_n)\\right)"); where
![x\_0=1](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=x_0%3D1&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"x_0=1")

![\\cos\\left(\\arctan(x)\\right) =
\\dfrac{1}{\\sqrt{1+x^2}}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Ccos%5Cleft%28%5Carctan%28x%29%5Cright%29+%3D+%5Cdfrac%7B1%7D%7B%5Csqrt%7B1%2Bx%5E2%7D%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\cos\\left(\\arctan(x)\\right) = \\dfrac{1}{\\sqrt{1+x^2}}")

![\\therefore x\_1=\\sqrt{\\dfrac{1}{2}}; \\; x\_2=
\\sqrt{\\dfrac{2}{3}}; \\; x\_3= \\sqrt{\\dfrac{3}{5}}; \\;
x\_4=\\sqrt{\\dfrac{5}{8}}...](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Ctherefore+x_1%3D%5Csqrt%7B%5Cdfrac%7B1%7D%7B2%7D%7D%3B+%5C%3B+x_2%3D+%5Csqrt%7B%5Cdfrac%7B2%7D%7B3%7D%7D%3B+%5C%3B+x_3%3D+%5Csqrt%7B%5Cdfrac%7B3%7D%7B5%7D%7D%3B+%5C%3B+x_4%3D%5Csqrt%7B%5Cdfrac%7B5%7D%7B8%7D%7D...&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\therefore x_1=\\sqrt{\\dfrac{1}{2}}; \\; x_2= \\sqrt{\\dfrac{2}{3}}; \\; x_3= \\sqrt{\\dfrac{3}{5}}; \\; x_4=\\sqrt{\\dfrac{5}{8}}...")

Given the mātrā-meru-paṅkti: ![f\[1\]=1, f\[2\]=2,
f\[n\]=f\[n-1\]+f\[n-2\];
x\_n=\\sqrt{\\dfrac{f\[n\]}{f\[n+1\]}}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=f%5B1%5D%3D1%2C+f%5B2%5D%3D2%2C+f%5Bn%5D%3Df%5Bn-1%5D%2Bf%5Bn-2%5D%3B+x_n%3D%5Csqrt%7B%5Cdfrac%7Bf%5Bn%5D%7D%7Bf%5Bn%2B1%5D%7D%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"f[1]=1, f[2]=2, f[n]=f[n-1]+f[n-2]; x_n=\\sqrt{\\dfrac{f[n]}{f[n+1]}}")

![\\displaystyle \\therefore \\lim\_{n \\to \\infty} x\_n=
\\sqrt{\\dfrac{1}{\\phi}}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Cdisplaystyle+%5Ctherefore+%5Clim_%7Bn+%5Cto+%5Cinfty%7D+x_n%3D+%5Csqrt%7B%5Cdfrac%7B1%7D%7B%5Cphi%7D%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\displaystyle \\therefore \\lim_{n \\to \\infty} x_n= \\sqrt{\\dfrac{1}{\\phi}}")

One can see that the
![x\_n](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=x_n&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"x_n") of this map corresponds to the bases of the right-side triangles
in successive steps (Figure 1), starting with ![\\overline{OA},
\\overline{OC\_1'}...\\overline{OK\_1}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BOA%7D%2C+%5Coverline%7BOC_1%27%7D...%5Coverline%7BOK_1%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{OA}, \\overline{OC_1'}...\\overline{OK_1}"). Thus,
![\\overline{OK\_1}=\\tfrac{1}{\\sqrt{\\phi}} \\;\\;\\;
\_{...\\blacksquare}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Coverline%7BOK_1%7D%3D%5Ctfrac%7B1%7D%7B%5Csqrt%7B%5Cphi%7D%7D+%5C%3B%5C%3B%5C%3B+_%7B...%5Cblacksquare%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"\\overline{OK_1}=\\tfrac{1}{\\sqrt{\\phi}} \\;\\;\\; _{...\\blacksquare}")

Now, we specify a similar map of the form:

![y\_{n+1}=\\sin\\left(\\arctan(y\_n)\\right)](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=y_%7Bn%2B1%7D%3D%5Csin%5Cleft%28%5Carctan%28y_n%29%5Cright%29&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0
"y_{n+1}=\\sin\\left(\\arctan(y_n)\\right)"); where
![y\_1=1](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=y_1%3D1&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0

"\\sin\\left(\\arctan(y)\\right) =\\tfrac{y}{\\sqrt{1+y^2}}"), we get

![y\_2=\\dfrac{1}{\\sqrt{2}}; \\; y\_3=\\dfrac{1}{\\sqrt{3}}; \\;

Thus, ![\\displaystyle \\lim\_{n \\to \\infty}
x\_n=0](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%5Cdisplaystyle+%5Clim_%7Bn+%5Cto+%5Cinfty%7D+x_n%3D0&bg=ffffff&fg=333333&s=0

Inspired by the above, we define the below intertwined maps that
generate interesting sequences and convergents:

"y_{n+1}=\\sin\\left(\\arctan\\left( x_{n+1}\\right)\\right)")
